TIMBER STRUCTURE INSPECTIONS

wood testing gives accurate data to help us stop more damage to the structure. Our timber inspections are done with compression waves that safely pass through the structure, instead of drilling many holes that speed up rotting. One of our specialties is the on-site, non-destructive testing of timber structures using our EPHOD™ process, a test used to see the timber structure’s health using compression waves, among other testing methods. With these tests and tools, our timber engineers and wood technicians build a repair and restore plan that is tailored to you. Sometimes, a core sample is needed. Using an increment borer a small sample is taken and sent to the lab to view the moisture levels in the wood. Although this would be considered destructive testing, we fill the hole. By filling the hole, we stop water and moisture from entering and starting to rot the structure.

TIMBER STRUCTURE ENGINEERING & DESIGN

After our timber inspection and lab tests, our engineers build. This report shows the the strong and weak parts of the timber structure. Our inspection reports show what part of the timber structure needs fixed. This also shows how one bad support can ruin your structure’s strength. Each area of the structure is given a rating from one to one hundred per cent based on our 40 years of timber inspection experience. Using this data, we form a plan with possible options for repair. With this comes a time frame of how soon each area will need to be fixed. Our engineers create working and construction detail plans. They also design new structures using top-of-the-line design software, that can be seen across the globe.

TIMBER QUALITY LAB TESTING

Our test lab and inspections are IAS certified. We have a wide range of wood testing techniques for large and small-scale projects. Core samples can be taken with increment borers that provide a sample that can be studied for rot. Our tests show heavy decay resulting from water flowing under nails. Our treatment prevents rot and damage and gives the wood a beautiful orange color. Glued-laminated timber (glulam) is layers of treated wood glued together. Our glulam is laced with Kevlar. It makes our glulam so strong, it’s bulletproof. This make for both a stylish and strong option for your timber structures. Core samples show the extent of the rot and provide lab samples. The lab samples are used to find rot and fungi. This gives an understanding of further risks. They are also used to look at the ratio of the woods’ density in water.
